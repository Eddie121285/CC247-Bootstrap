What About My Insurance?

Lots of insurance policies either don’t have a courtesy car included in them as standard, or can be often subject to hidden charges or insurance excess payments, make sure you have studied your documentation to avoid any of these hidden charges.

Within your insurance documents there could be a 'subject to availability' clause in their. This means you're not guaranteed a courtesy car, and could end up on a waiting list without a vehicle. If your car is written off or stolen, many standard policies won't give you a courtesy car as the clause only covers you for repairs.

You may also have a fixed 'expiration date' depending on your policy. It is important that you check for this clause, you dont want to be left with out a car if the repairs take longer than expected.

More often than not the car provided from your insurer isn’t a like for like model and can be a significant downgrade on your original vehicle.

If you have a basic courtesy car policy, you're likely to get a 1 litre, 5-door compact car as standard, no matter what car you were driving before.

Most insurances will refuse a Courtesy Car in the event of a write off, as Car Hire Cover is required in these cases and is often overlooked when taking the policy out.

Don’t feel like you have nowhere to turn because your insurance won’t help or you can’t afford your excess payments.

Remember you are not obligated to take a courtesy car from your isurer so dont be pressured in to any arrangement with them.

Courtesy Car After Write-Off

If the insurer deems your car as a write-off, you might not get a courtesy car. You risk being without a car altogether unless your policy has hire car cover. This isn’t normally included with comprehensive policies. It’s usually an add-on.

Credit hire cars are normally closer to the value of your own car, and as a result they can seem like a better deal. But you need be careful before signing up for a credit hire car – the charge will be in your name until the insurer of the other party accepts liability.

If they don't you'll be left with the cost of the car hire, which could be anywhere from £30 a day to over £1,000. Because of the high costs many customers are now stuck with, the Association of British Insurers (ABI) publish a set of rules outlining the maximum rates for car hires.

If you find yourself without a vehicle after an accident, replacement cars can be a lifesaver. But knowing what expect from your courtesy car policy, as well as the difference between courtesy and a credit hire car, can protect you against any unnecessary costs or surprises.
